Due to the recent changes in Google Play Policies made on 16/12/2020, we have observed a change of behavior in the review and approval initiatives of Google Play. You can see the policy updates here.

Although there were no policy changes made covering this scope, policy reviewers started to send warnings about collection of Installed Applications information starting from 17/12/2020.

We have immediately released a new Android SDK version (11.6.1) which does not include any methods related to Installed Applications. We have also updated React Native and Flutter SDKs. If you are using an older version of the SDK, it’s recommended to update the SDK and ensure you have followed the steps explained below.

This guide is prepared to help you update all necessary APKs listed in your Play Store account and get an approval.

1.Updating/Removing Old Tracks from the All Stages        

Before starting the release process, please make sure to remove (if possible)  the non-compliant APK(s) or old versions of your apps on all stages including Alpha, Beta and production. During the review process, Google may reject or remove your application if you have any non-compliant APK(s) in your internal tracks.

For support on deactivating the non-compliant APK, please try the following steps below:

  1. To deactivate a non-compliant APK, create a new release and upload a compliant APK to the same track.
  2. Be sure to increment the APK version number and set the release to 100% rollout, in order to successfully override and deactivate the non-compliant APK(s).

  1. Sign in to your Play Console, upload the modified policy compliant APK across all tracks, and deactivate the non-compliant APK(s).

For your reference, if you continue to have technical issues related to deactivating the non-compliant APK, please contact the Google Play developer support team.

3.Location Collection

According to Google’s policy restrictions, you may only request permissions that are crucial to the core functionality of your application. You may not use permissions that give access to the user or device data for undisclosed, unimplemented, or disallowed features or purposes.

If you’re collecting location information you need to be sure that you follow these steps :

  1. Request location permissions : https://developer.android.com/training/location/permissions#background 
  2. Location updates in Android 11 : https://developer.android.com/about/versions/11/privacy/location#background-location 

If your application collects the location information to use the Geofence feature via the Insider SDK, you can exclude the following permissions in your app’s AndroidManifest.xml

<uses-permission

       android:name="android.permission.ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION"

       tools:node="remove"/>

   <uses-permission

       android:name="android.permission.ACCESS_BACKGROUND_LOCATION"

       tools:node="remove" />

   <uses-permission

       android:name="android.permission.ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION"

       tools:node="remove" />

 In the scenario where you want to stop collecting a specific location collection (e.g background), you just need to remove the corresponding permission method only.

Please note that; if the application is compliant to collect location permissions, that is, if you meet the requirements Google requires to collect location; no need to remove any method/permissions of the Geofence feature.

4.Privacy Policy Review

From April 2020 google is on track to enforce user’s right to give consent / reject any kind of information that can be tracked.

If your application or your other SDKs uses/collects any information without EXPLICITLY declaring it, you should update your privacy policy immediately about these data collections.
